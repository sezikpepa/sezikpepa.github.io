Me as a learner

Whenever I study or do assignments I do it spontaneously, I do not plan a concrete structure of my day or week, but I rather do tasks when I feel I am in correct mood for that work. Moreover, with approaching deadlines I dedicate more time to that specific task. I can see some flaws in this technique, but so far I worked great for me. Everytime I do something, I do it on 100 %, so I finish my work on time but I need specific deadlines because the pressure of time gives me motivation to continue on the work.

Best techniques which always worked for me during studying sessions is studying with visual aids. With a grain of salt, whenever I see something I will never forget it, because I see because the layout, colors and patterns will always stick in my head. This includes, graphs, videos, picture and many other forms. To learn practical things I always stand for opinion that to learn specific task it is necessary to do that specific task. For example it is not possible to learn how to programm by listening to lectures about programming, but it is important to sit behind the computer and try to make a computer program and use lectures and videos as a source of help when new problem arrise during programming.

Presentation for interdisciplinary project

My part of the presentation was about the explanation of prescription cascades and PSSA. Before I went to the presentation I asked my girlfriend to give me the feedback on the presentation. She pointed out that I should use more visual aids, that the part about cascades was understandable, but picture would help. The same applied for the PSSA. According to this feedback I created the visual aids for these topics.

During the presentation I felt that the explanation of the topic was pretty clear. For the part about cascades the visual aid really helped and the the continuous adding of another parts of visual aid helped a lot. For the other part I felt it could be improved and as it is the difficult part about the whole project even for us, I should have taken more time practicing the explanation as it is not an easy topic at all. Furthermore, I wrote a lot of speaker notes for the presentation, but I found it difficult to present and look at the notes, as it is really distructing. Next time I would like to try implement these notes into the presentation itself, so it would blend with the slides and I could use the slides as a structure not only for listeners but also for me. During this presentation I asked Marieke Spreeuwenberg to give me the feedback via the questions I provided. Following quote is my attempt for summirization of the feedback.

"Overall presentation was good, visual aids were helpful, confusion about the goal of the project, difficult to understand PSSA, wiggling (body language)."

Even though, I added visual aids it helped only for the part about prescribing cascades and the visual aid for PSSA did not helped much. What I am taking from this part is, that it is important to select proper visualization, as not every visualization is not suitable for given problem. It is just a simple statement from me, but I know that I will give it more time in the future. Also I will focus more on the main message of the presentation, maybe to always create a slide in the beginning. Regardless the wiggling and standing behind the desk, I also noticed it a little bit, but it is hard to switch between slides and actively explaning something in front of the projector. For simple slide show it is possible to ask for help of a collegue, but for switching of visual aid every few seconds it is not sustainable. For this purpose I was searching how to solve this issue and discovered a tool called presenter and bought myself one. This tool will enable me to stand in front of my slides and actively change them without the need of going to the laptop. I will try to use it in the next presentations in following periods. Also I will focus more on actively looking on the crowd.
