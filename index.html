Why I chose HDT

Me as a learner

Whenever I study or do assignments I do it spontaneously, I do not plan a concrete structure for my day or week, but I rather do tasks when I feel I am in the correct mood for that work. Moreover, with approaching deadlines, I dedicate more time to that specific task. I can see some flaws in this technique, but so far it worked great for me. Every time I do something, I do it on 100%, so I always finish my work on time but I need specific deadlines because the pressure of time gives me motivation to continue on the work.

The best technique that has always worked for me during studying sessions is studying with visual aids. With a grain of salt, whenever I see something I will never forget. Layouts, colors and patterns will always stick in my head. This includes graphs, videos, pictures and many other forms. To learn practical things I always stand for the opinion that to learn a specific task it is necessary to do that specific task. For example, it is not possible to learn how to program by listening to lectures about programming, but it is important to sit behind the computer and try to make a computer program and use lectures and videos as a source of help when new problems arise during programming.

Presentation for interdisciplinary project

My part of the presentation was about the explanation of prescription cascades and PSSA. Before I went to the presentation I had asked my girlfriend for feedback on the presentation. She pointed out that I should use more visual aids, that the part about cascades was understandable, but picture would help. The same applied for the PSSA. According to this feedback I created the visual aids for these topics.

During the presentation I felt that the explanation of the topic was pretty clear. For the part about cascades the visual aid really helped and the continuous adding of another parts of visual aid helped a lot. For the other part I felt it could be improved and as it is the difficult part about the whole project, even for us, I should have taken more time practicing the explanation as it is not an easy topic at all. Furthermore, I wrote a lot of speaker notes for the presentation, but I found it difficult to present and to look at the notes at once, as it is really distracting. Next time I would like to try implement these notes into the presentation itself, so it would blend with the slides and I could use the slides as a structure not only for listeners but also for me. During this presentation I asked Marieke Spreeuwenberg to give me the feedback via the questions I provided. Following quote is my attempt of summarization of the feedback.

"Overall presentation was good, visual aids were helpful, confusion about the goal of the project, difficult to understand PSSA, wiggling (body language)."

Even though, I added visual aids it helped only for the part about prescribing cascades and the visual aid for PSSA did not helped much. What I am taking from this part is, that it is important to select proper visualization, as not every visualization is not suitable for given problem. It is just a simple statement from me, but I know that I will give it more time in the future. Also I will focus more on the main message of the presentation, maybe to always create a slide in the beginning about the goals and structure. Regardless the wiggling and standing behind the desk, I also noticed it a little bit, but it is hard to switch between slides and actively explaining something in front of the projector. For simple slideshow it is possible to ask for help of a colleague. On the other hand, it is not suitable for switching the visual aids every few seconds as it can distract the flow of the presentation. For this purpose I was searching how to solve this issue and discovered a tool called "presenter" and bought myself one. This tool will enable me to stand in front of my slides and actively change them without the need of going to the laptop. I will try to use it in the next presentations in following periods. Also I will focus more on actively looking on the crowd.

In second period I continued in pursing the goals. The most important and biggest step was conducting a workshop about SQL.

Expert

Before creating the first workshop I wanted to approach few people to ask them about their experience how to conduct workshops but then I realized that I would like to create my own style. Also I could get a lot of biases from their point of view. Moreover, I am a student now, so I know what I like and what I don't like. So I focused on my experience from different kind of workshops and tried to recreate them.

As my workshop was not part of a regular schedule it was important to find a place and time for the workshop. Place was quite simple, as I was able to reserve a room at university campus. For the time I used when2meet.com website. During my bachelor we used this a lot I had a great experience with that. With the help of this site I was able to find a timeslot, when 9 of 11 people had free time - survey This was a great choice because when I looked on other dates, even thought a lot of options was provided there were not so successful. During the first two month I had two main problems related to technical subjects and workshops in general.

  1. Workshops are like normal lectures followed by long practical part
  2. There is a lot of talking in general but people cannot use it

Whenever I had a workshop during master studies it was quite difficult to follow tasks which were given, as I did not correctly remember everything what was said. Also as the tasks were long without showing "correct" answers it was hard to adapt. Also some workshops lacked the practical part. Moreover tutorials about Technology in Healthcare were about "talking about technologies" which does not help at all in understanding the topic - as everybody can use a lot of fancy words without understanding the topic. These were two main points I wanted to improve in my first workshop. I created a presentation about the topic which supported my workshop and I tried to focus on a practical engagement from the first second which would be done by small pieces. Everything which I explained was immediately done in a practical manner. I tried to talk about a topic for maximum 3 minutes. This worked perfectly. Everybody managed to solve all exercises without any problems. Before the workshop I thought that it will be hard for people to understand. The result of the workshop surprised me. Attendees were able to learn basics of SQL and in my opinion it is more than half of knowledge about SQL, which they will ever need in their life as medical professionals or researches.

I also conducted a feedback form to get a point of view from the other side. Overall average mark was 4.87 out of 5, which exceeded my expectations and I am glad that people enjoyed that. Moreover, it pointed out, that practical part worked and people learned a lot and there was a nice atmosphere to ask questions and dive more into the details. On the other hand it also pointed out things which could be better, I was also talking with people after the workshop, so not everything is in the form. In the next workshop I would like to improve settings into the context and how can be SQL used with other technologies - for example how can we access and ask database from python with help of SQL. The other thing was to add more different activities to the workshop, as sometimes people forgot the name of the keyword or order of commands. It would be nice to include random questions for example via Kahoot. This would change to flow of the workshop a little bit help people to solidify the knowledge.

Overall I am satisfied with workshop and I am glad that me thinking process how to make the workshop great worked and I will stick to that. Of course, I will take also take possible improvements into account and I will try to use them in the next workshop.
